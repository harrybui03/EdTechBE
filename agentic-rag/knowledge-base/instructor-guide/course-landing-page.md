# Setting Up Your Course Landing Page

## Overview

The course landing page is what potential students see when browsing courses. A well-crafted landing page significantly improves enrollment rates.

## Accessing Landing Page Settings

1. Navigate to your course editing page (`/instructor/courses/{courseId}/edit`)
2. Click on "Course landing page" in the navigation menu
3. You'll see all landing page configuration options

## Required Information

### Course Title

- **Purpose**: The main title of your course
- **Requirements**: Must be clear, descriptive, and engaging
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use action words (Learn, Master, Build)
  - Include the main topic or skill
  - Keep it concise (under 60 characters)
  - Example: "Learn Photoshop CS6 from Scratch"

### Course Subtitle

- **Purpose**: A brief tagline that appears below the title
- **Requirements**: Optional but highly recommended
- **Best Practices**:
  - Summarize the value proposition
  - Highlight key benefits
  - Keep it short and compelling

### Course Description

- **Purpose**: Detailed information about what students will learn
- **Requirements**: Required for a complete landing page
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use clear, engaging language
  - List learning objectives
  - Highlight what makes your course unique
  - Include who the course is for
  - Use formatting (headings, lists, bold) for readability

## Visual Elements

### Course Thumbnail

- **Purpose**: The main image representing your course
- **Requirements**: Recommended size 1280x720 pixels
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use high-quality, relevant images
  - Include course title or key visual elements
  - Ensure text is readable at thumbnail size
  - Use consistent branding

### Promotional Video

- **Purpose**: A preview video that introduces your course
- **Requirements**: Optional but highly recommended
- **Best Practices**:
  - Keep it under 2 minutes
  - Introduce yourself and the course
  - Show course highlights
  - Use YouTube or other video platform URLs
  - Ensure good audio and video quality

## Course Metadata

### Language

Select the primary language of your course:
- Vietnamese
- English
- Chinese
- Other supported languages

### Tags

- **Purpose**: Help students discover your course
- **How to Add**:
  1. Type a tag name in the tag input field
  2. Press Enter or click "Add"
  3. Tags appear as chips that can be removed
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use relevant, specific tags
  - Include skill names, tools, and topics
  - Don't over-tag (5-10 tags is usually sufficient)

### Labels (Subjects)

- **Purpose**: Categorize your course by subject area
- **How to Add**: Similar to tags, type and add labels
- **Best Practices**:
  - Use broad subject categories
  - Examples: "Programming", "Design", "Business"

## Saving Changes

1. After making changes, click "Save" or "Update Course"
2. Wait for the success confirmation
3. Use "Preview" to see how your landing page looks to students

## Preview Your Landing Page

1. Click the "Preview" button (eye icon)
2. Review how your course appears in the catalog
3. Check all elements display correctly
4. Make adjustments as needed

## Best Practices for Landing Pages

### Writing Effective Descriptions

- **Start with a Hook**: Capture attention in the first sentence
- **List Learning Outcomes**: Use bullet points for clarity
- **Include Prerequisites**: Help students know if the course is right for them
- **Show Value**: Explain what students will gain
- **Use Social Proof**: Mention your expertise or credentials

### Visual Appeal

- **Professional Thumbnail**: Invest in a good course image
- **Consistent Branding**: Use colors and style that match your teaching brand
- **High-Quality Video**: A polished preview video builds trust

### SEO and Discoverability

- **Relevant Tags**: Use tags that students actually search for
- **Clear Title**: Include keywords students might search
- **Complete Description**: More complete information helps search algorithms

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Vague Titles**: "My Course" doesn't tell students anything
- **Missing Thumbnail**: Courses without images get less attention
- **Poor Description**: Too short or too long descriptions reduce conversions
- **Irrelevant Tags**: Tags that don't match content confuse students
- **No Preview Video**: Missing an opportunity to connect with students

## Testing Your Landing Page

Before publishing, ask yourself:

1. Is the title clear and compelling?
2. Does the description explain what students will learn?
3. Is the thumbnail attractive and professional?
4. Does the preview video showcase the course well?
5. Are tags relevant and helpful for discovery?
6. Would you enroll based on this landing page?
