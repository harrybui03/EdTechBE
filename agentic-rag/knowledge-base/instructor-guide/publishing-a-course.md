# Publishing Your Course

## Overview

After creating your course content, you need to publish it to make it available to students. This guide covers the publishing process and requirements.

## Prerequisites for Publishing

Before you can publish your course, ensure you have:

1. **At least 1 chapter**: Your course must have at least one chapter
2. **At least 1 lesson**: Each chapter should contain at least one lesson
3. **Complete course information**: Fill in all required fields in the course editing sections

## Course Status

Your course can be in one of these states:

- **Draft**: Course is being created/edited, not visible to students
- **Published**: Course is live and available for enrollment

## Publishing Process

### Step 1: Complete Course Setup

Navigate through all course editing sections:

1. **Intended Learners**: Define who your course is for
2. **Course Structure**: Add chapters and organize content
3. **Film & Edit**: Upload or link course videos
4. **Curriculum**: Create and organize lessons
5. **Caption** (optional): Add video captions for accessibility
6. **Accessibility** (optional): Configure accessibility features
7. **Landing Page**: Set up course title, description, thumbnail, and promotional video
8. **Pricing**: Set course price (0 for free courses)
9. **Promotions** (optional): Create discount codes

### Step 2: Review Course Content

1. Use the "Preview" button to review your course
2. Check that all content displays correctly
3. Verify that lessons are properly organized
4. Ensure pricing and landing page information is accurate

### Step 3: Publish the Course

1. Navigate to the course editing page
2. Look for the "Publish" button (usually in the top-right area)
3. Click "Publish Course"
4. Confirm the publishing action in the modal dialog
5. Wait for the system to process your request

### Step 4: Verify Publication

After publishing:

1. You'll see a success message
2. The course status will change to "Published"
3. Your course will be visible in the course catalog
4. Students can now enroll in your course

## Important Notes

- **Cannot Unpublish**: Once published, you cannot unpublish a course (check current platform behavior)
- **Edit After Publishing**: You can still edit course content after publishing
- **Status Indicator**: The course status is clearly displayed in the editing interface

## Troubleshooting

### "Cannot Publish" Error

If you see an error when trying to publish:

1. **Check Course Structure**: Ensure you have at least 1 chapter and 1 lesson
2. **Verify Required Fields**: Make sure all mandatory fields are filled
3. **Review Error Message**: The system will indicate what's missing
4. **Complete Missing Sections**: Go back and complete any incomplete sections

### Course Still Shows as Draft

If your course remains in Draft status:

1. Refresh the page
2. Check if the publish action completed successfully
3. Verify course structure requirements are met
4. Contact support if the issue persists

## After Publishing

Once your course is published:

- **Monitor Enrollments**: Track how many students enroll
- **Respond to Reviews**: Engage with student feedback
- **Update Content**: Continue improving your course based on student needs
- **Promote Your Course**: Share your course link to attract students

## Best Practices

- **Complete All Sections**: Fill out all course information for better discoverability
- **Test Before Publishing**: Preview everything to ensure quality
- **Set Appropriate Pricing**: Research similar courses to set competitive prices
- **Create Engaging Landing Page**: Make your course attractive to potential students
- **Add Promotional Video**: A good preview video increases enrollment rates
